Tips for Digital Success

  • Verify Information: Before sharing or relying on information, cross-reference it across multiple reputable sources.
  • Check Author Credentials: Look for who wrote the article or created the content. Are they an expert in the field?
  • Be Aware of Bias: All sources have some perspective. Understand the source's potential biases and motivations.
  • Check Publication Dates: Ensure the information is current and relevant to your needs. Old data might not apply to current situations.
  • Evaluate Citations: Good sources cite their information. If claims lack citations, be skeptical.
  • Use Primary Sources: When possible, go to the original source rather than reading about what someone else said about it.
  • Question Sensational Headlines: Be cautious of exaggerated claims. Good journalism aims for accuracy over drama.
  • Protect Your Privacy: Be mindful of what personal information you share on social media and with websites.

Keep Learning

Digital literacy is an ongoing journey. Stay curious, keep questioning, and continue developing your skills in evaluating and navigating the vast information landscape of the internet. Remember: the most informed citizens are those who think critically about their sources!
